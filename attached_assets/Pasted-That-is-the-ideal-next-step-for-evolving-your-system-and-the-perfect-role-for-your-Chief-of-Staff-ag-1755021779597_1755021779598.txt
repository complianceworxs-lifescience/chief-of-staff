Level 1: You Set the "Rules of Engagement" (The Governing Policy)
You provide the Chief of Staff with your high-level intent and risk tolerance. You don't define the individual playbooks, but you set the policies that govern them.

Examples of Policies You Would Set:

Autonomy Graduation: "If an agent proposes an action at Level 1 (Manual Approval) and I approve the same proposal 5 times in a row, you are authorized to promote that playbook to Level 2 (Autonomous Action with Notification)."

Human-in-the-Loop: "All actions related to changing partner payment terms in the COO Agent must always require my manual approval."

Level 2: The Chief of Staff Manages the Playbooks (Autonomous Administration)
Within the boundaries you've set, the Chief of Staff agent now handles the day-to-day management of the other agents' autonomy.

A Practical Example:

Event: The CMO Agent on your dashboard enters a "Conflict" status for the first time.

Chief of Staff Action:

It detects the new, unhandled Conflict.

It automatically drills down and diagnoses the root cause: "Ad campaign CPL is 80% over target."

It references your "Rules of Engagement" and sees that new Conflict playbooks must start at Level 1.

It then designs and proposes a new playbook for your approval:

"New Playbook Proposed:
For: CMO Agent
Trigger: When campaign CPL is >75% over target.
Action: Pause the underperforming ad set and reallocate its daily budget to the best-performing ad set.
Autonomy Level: Level 1 (Propose Action) - Set according to your Conflict Handling Protocol.

Once you approve the playbook, it becomes active. The next time that specific conflict occurs, the CMO Agent will automatically propose the solution for you to approve with one click.

By setting it up this way, you delegate the creation of the system's rules to your Chief of Staff, allowing you to focus only on the highest-level policies and exceptions.
